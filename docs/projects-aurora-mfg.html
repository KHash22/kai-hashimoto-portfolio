SOLAR CAR MFG & DFM

HEAD OF MANUFACTURING

The construction of the solar car is a team effort. In addition to taking thousands of combined hours from the team, it demands an effective allocation of time and resources.

As Team Manager, I also organized and led a smaller ten-person team to fabicate of the composite chassis and shell of the solar car, as well as conduct system-wide design reviews on manufacturability and assembly. Some of my achievements include:

  • Performed highly detailed estimation of composite weights and enforced strict CAD adhereance to achieve a 0.8 kg delta between intended CAD design vs true vehicle weight. Previous generation vehicle had an 30 kg + difference.
  • Secured 106'000 USD in equivalent in-kind value, such as composite fabrics and CNC services towards the construction of the solar car.
  • Prepared Engineering Drawings to ASMEY14.5 Standard for core suspension components (Upright, LCA). Saved 2500 USD in potential re-orders by performing reviews on engienering drawings of team members and catching improperly toleranced features.
  • Orchestrated the manufacturing process of the Aurora solar car: procurement, scheduling, composite fabrication (non-exhaustive list).

FROM DESIGN TO MANUFACTURING

Before begining the physical fabrication of the car, practical elements to integrate the rest of the solar car's other features, such as the solar array, wheels, and view window must be modified into the design. I partitioned out features such as indentations for the solar cells, split lines for the upper/lower halves of the fiberglass molds, and service doors for the wheels. Because of the System Requirements defined during the conceptual design process, the placement of these features were already well-defined / considered for in advance of this step.

The car's aeroshell is typically constructed of Carbon Fiber Composites and kevlar to keep the vehicle weight low as possible. This means to make this car manufacturable, I partitioned the surfaces of the aeroshell into 6 segments + view window. These surfaces will then be used to machine a positive plug before making a negative mold. Onwards to fabricating the car!

PLUG ➞ MOLD ➞ PART

A solar car typically starts out as a positive plug. This is then turned into a fiberglass mold, then an infusion layup is done to construct the final part. A heartfelt thank you to Jeff Gatland of Fiberglass Unlimited, for his incredible mentorship, guidance, and support for the team.

A stack-up of tooling foam. A CNC will then form this into a plug.

Because the car is almost 5m long, plugs must be built in halves.

LOGISTICS WINS WARS: I put a strong emphasis on thetimely delivery of components to ensure minimal downtime. Being efficient with time is a must when building prototype vehicle in just 8 months.

If it hasn't been already made abundant, I am VERY hands-on, and like to have a deep understanding of every level of an opeation.

CAD FIDELITY: By both accurately estimating the weight of composite parts and strict adherenace to CAD, our chassis layup achieved 95.5% parity by mass. The high-quality construction of the chassis was necessary for the complex suspension clevis designs, needed towards an internal target to reduce suspension weight by 20% compared to the previous-generation vehicle.
