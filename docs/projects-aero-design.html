AERO DESIGN / SIMULATION

MOTIVATING PROBLEM

At speeds above 60 km/h, aerodynamic forces can account for ~70% of drag acting on a solar car. It is imperative energy losses by aerodynamics is kept to a minimum. Recall, aerodynamic energy cost is proportional to the cube of the speed! The goal of a good design then becomes to minimize CdA (drag coefficient) both in a straightline and across a range of sideward winds.

For our solar car, this is achieved with a super-streamlined 'aeroshell' to encapsulate the vehicle, doubling as the primary surface to which the solar cells are mounted to. However, this shell must be designed in way to obey the constraints of Manufacturability, Interference, Ergonomics, and Regulatory Compliance. There are over 80+ System Requirements for the aeroshell alone, with examples such as:

  • Draftability: The upper and lower halves of the car must have a minimum 3° draft for composites fabrication.
  • Unobstructed View: a driver must have 100° of sight to either side of the car.
  • Solar Collector Area: Each car is allowed up to 4 square meters of solar cells.
  • Front Daytime Running Lights: Must be located at least 400mm apart, and no further back than 200mm from tip of the car.

For the Aurora Project, I was principaly responsible for the design and fabrication of the aeroshell.

AIRFOIL SELECTION

Without having to run a complex 3D CFD simulations, the design of the aeroshell starts with a selection of a known airfoil shape. Common options include NACA6-Series, or by PARSEC parametrization. While in practice it's quite difficult to translate the observed flowfield in 2D to a 3D car (given the <0.5 aspect ratio), this gives me a starting point to further refine the design from.

CAD, SIM, VALIDATE, REPEAT

The team only had 2 months to refine an aeroshell before manufacturing begins. Each iteration is simulated in CFD, studied, then iterated upon using the information available from simulation. I used 3DEXPERIENCE ENOVIA PRODUCT LIFECYCLE MANAGEMENT to perform revision control on over 40+ iterations of the aeroshell design.

I used tools like Isophote Maps help to spot surface imperfections, as creating smooth, continous surfaces is significant as to not increase the energy required for the air to move around the car. On the otherhand, Draft Analysis helps ensure the part can be removed from the fiberglass mold after laying up the composites.
